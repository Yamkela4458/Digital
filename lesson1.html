Lesson Plan: English Grammar and Punctuation

Grade 3 | CAPS Aligned | Language & Literacies

Lesson Overview

This lesson introduces learners to basic grammar and punctuation rules in English. Students will explore the correct use of capital letters, full stops, commas, and question marks through engaging reading and writing activities.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and use capital letters correctly in sentences.
  • Recognize and apply full stops, commas, and question marks appropriately.
  • Construct simple sentences using correct grammar and punctuation.

Teaching Resources

  • Whiteboard and markers
  • Printed worksheet: “Punctuation Practice”
  • Short paragraph cards for editing
  • Interactive board or projector (if available)

Lesson Activities

  • Warm-up (5 mins): Display a few unpunctuated sentences and let learners correct them orally.
  • Guided Activity (15 mins): Review punctuation rules using examples on the board.
  • Group Task (20 mins): Learners work in pairs to rewrite a short paragraph using correct punctuation.
  • Independent Practice (10 mins): Each learner completes the “Punctuation Practice” worksheet.
  • Plenary (5 mins): Discuss answers and highlight common mistakes.

Assessment

  • Observe learner participation during oral corrections.
  • Mark worksheets for correct use of punctuation.
  • Provide verbal feedback and corrections in class.

Teacher Notes

Reinforce the importance of punctuation in clear communication. Encourage learners to read their sentences aloud to check if punctuation helps meaning. Use fun punctuation games for reinforcement.
